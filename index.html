A Journey Built for Two

Europe isn't just a different continent; it's a different rhythm of life. For your first trip, we aren't just sending you to see buildings. We are sending you to experience the Renaissance in person, to feel the weight of the Cold War in the streets of Prague, and to witness the sheer audacity of the Hapsburg Empire in Vienna.

You’ll start in the comfort of family at Ramstein, then sprint into the glamour of Paris, before finally surrendering to the slow, golden flow of the Danube. This is your story—one where you never have to worry about the details, only the memories.

Your Day as Royalty

Imagine a place so opulent that it actually bankrupted a nation. Versailles is not just a building; it is a dream made of gold, marble, and mirrors. It was designed to overwhelm you, to make you feel like the center of the universe, and today, it serves as the ultimate backdrop for your Parisian adventure.

01

The Hall of Mirrors

This is the crown jewel. Seventeen mirror-clad arches reflect the seventeen arcade windows that look out onto the gardens. As the afternoon sun hits this room, the entire hall glows in a way that feels supernatural. You aren't just walking down a hallway; you are walking through the center of 17th-century power where treaties were signed and empires were decided.

02

The Queen's Hamlet

Walk past the massive fountains and you’ll find Marie Antoinette’s private escape. It’s a fairytale village with thatched roofs and a working farm. It is the quietest, most intimate part of the estate—where the Queen went to escape the suffocating rules of the palace. It’s a place for slow, quiet walks and the best photos of the trip.

First-Timer Wow Factor

Don't just look at the palace—feel the scale of it. Rent a golf cart or a rowboat on the Grand Canal. Watch the "Musical Fountains" where the water dances to 300-year-old compositions. It is the only place on earth where the gardening is as famous as the architecture. You will want to stay until they close the gates.

Part I: The Ground Tour

THE ROAD TO THE RIVER

01-02

The Crossing

Detroit to the Rhein

Your journey begins at DTW, boarding the Delta non-stop to Frankfurt. You’ll cross the Atlantic while you sleep, landing in the morning air of Germany. The Goulds will be there to greet you, whisking you away to their home in Kaiserslautern. This is your "soft landing"—a chance to enjoy a quiet German dinner, catch your breath, and let the reality of being in Europe finally sink in.

03-05

Military & Medieval

Ramstein & The River Valleys

You'll get an insider’s look at Ramstein Air Base—a modern city of its own—before diving into the "Old World." We’ll drive the winding roads of the Moselle and Rhine valleys, where every bend in the river reveals another 800-year-old castle. You'll walk the cobblestones of Bernkastel-Kues, a town that looks exactly like the setting of a Disney movie, and taste Riesling grown on the steepest slopes in the world.

06-09

The Golden City

Parisian Nights

After a high-speed train ride through the French countryside, you arrive in Paris. For four days, the city is yours. You'll marvel at the glass pyramid of the Louvre, stand in awe beneath the Arc de Triomphe, and witness the Eiffel Tower sparkle on the hour. Between the monuments, you’ll discover the magic of a Parisian bistro—where the bread is perfect, the wine is cheap, and time doesn't seem to matter.

10-13

The Heart of Bohemia

Prague: A Story in Stone

Prague is often called the most beautiful city in Europe because it survived WWII untouched. You'll walk the Charles Bridge, lined with statues of saints, and climb the hill to Prague Castle—the largest ancient castle complex in the world. At night, the Old Town Square feels like stepping back 500 years. It’s a city of towers, puppets, and a history that is as dark as it is beautiful.

Your Home on the Water

The Amadeus isn't just a boat; it's a sanctuary. From the sun deck where you can watch the vineyards go by with a cocktail, to the panoramic restaurant where every meal is a gala event, the ship is designed for relaxation. No packing, no traffic—just the gentle movement of the river.

  • ✓ Gourmet Dining: Regionally inspired menus and complimentary wines from the valley you just sailed through.
Day 14

Embarkation: Passau

Known as the "City of Three Rivers," Passau is where the Inn and the Ilz flow into the Danube. You'll board the Amadeus, meet your crew, and enjoy your first gourmet dinner as the ship slips away from the dock. The adventure on the water has officially begun.

Day 15

Regensburg, Germany

Walk through the Porta Praetoria—the gate to a Roman fort built in 179 AD. Regensburg is one of Germany’s best-preserved medieval cities. You’ll visit the Old Stone Bridge and perhaps enjoy a sausage at the "Historische Wurstküche," the oldest sausage kitchen in the world.

Day 16

Linz & Salzburg

Cross into Austria and arrive in Linz. From here, you’ll journey to Salzburg—home of Mozart. You’ll walk through the Mirabell Gardens and see the fortress overlooking the city before returning to the ship for an evening sail into the heart of the Alps.

Day 17

The Wachau Valley

The most beautiful stretch of the river. You'll visit Melk Abbey, a massive golden monastery on a cliff. Then, the ship sails through the valley—a UNESCO site famous for its apricots and vineyards—stopping in the blue-towered village of Dürnstein.

The Finale

The Grand Arrival: Budapest

There is no sight in Europe more spectacular than arriving in Budapest by ship at night. The Parliament building, the Chain Bridge, and Buda Castle are all illuminated in gold. You'll have two days here to soak in the thermal baths and witness a view that will take your breath away. It is the perfect, cinematic end to your journey.
